Shared Task on Original Research Title Reconstruction and Retrieval from LLM-Generated Reformulations

Task Overview

Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly used to rewrite and rephrase scientific content for different audiences and communication purposes. While these reformulations improve accessibility and engagement, they also create challenges for content attribution, provenance tracking, and source recovery. This shared task focuses on reconstructing and retrieving original research paper titles from LLM-generated reformulations. The dataset consists of original research paper titles and their corresponding rewritten variants generated under three controlled stylistic cues: Technical, Accessible, and Catchy. For each original title, ten reformulations are generated for each style, resulting in thirty variants that preserve the core research idea while modifying the presentation style.

Subtasks

Subtask 1: Original Title Reconstruction

Participants are provided with a reformulated title and its style cue (Technical, Accessible, or Catchy). The goal is to reconstruct the most probable original research title. This task evaluates a system's ability to recover source content despite stylistic transformations introduced by LLMs.

Subtask 2: Original Title Retrieval and Ranking

Participants must produce a ranked list of up to ten candidate original titles ordered by their likelihood of being the true source title. This ranking-based task evaluates semantic matching and source-title identification across diverse stylistic rewrites.

Why This Task Matters

The task has applications in scientific information retrieval, content provenance, academic search systems, duplicate detection, citation linking, and AI-generated text analysis. It contributes to understanding how much source information remains recoverable after controlled LLM-based rewriting and provides insights into the traceability and attribution of AI-generated scientific content.
